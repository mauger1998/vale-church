welcome AT VALE CHURCH

And that includes you

Vale Church is an inclusive church of moderate Catholic tradition in the Church of England

I have been Rector at St Michel du Valle, known simply as the Vale Church, since February 2023, The church has been witnessing to the Christian faith and serving the people of the Vale for over 1000 years. I feel very honoured to be part of this tradition as I get to know the people of the parish of the Vale, the congregation and the many people who attend our Sunday services but also those who attend weddings, baptisms and funerals here or simply visit as they pass by.

Bev Hervé (Rector)

OUR MISSION

Create a welcoming and nurturing space where individuals from all walks of life can come together to explore, celebrate and deepen their spiritual journey

We invite you to join us for a worship experience that brings together hearts and minds

Immerse yourself in the profound impact of worship at Vale Parish Church. Embrace the joy of congregating in our gatherings that elevate souls, ignite passions, and nurture profound bonds. Join us for our routine services or unique occasions, and be a part of a tapestry of devotion, contemplation, and togetherness.

Explore the array of worship experiences awaiting you at Vale Parish Curch.

Sunday services

8.00am | Said Eucharist

A quiet celebration of the Eucharist in traditional language. This service has no hymns and lasts around 30 minutes.

10.30am | Parish Eucharist

Our main weekly act of worship where we gather as a community to hear God’s word and worship together. This service includes hymns, organ and choir, and lasts for just over an hour. Refreshments are served afterwards.

10.30am | Family Eucharist (Third Sunday of every month)

This is a more relaxed Sunday service intended for people of all ages. Younger members of the community are more involved in this service, which lasts for around an hour and is followed by refreshments.

Weekday services

8.30am | Tuesday & Thursday (not 1st Tues of the month)

Every Tuesday & Thursday (not 1st Tues of the month) at 8.30am we gather for Morning Prayer. This is a quiet time to be led in devotional prayer and is a wonderful way to focus your time and attention on seeking God's plan for the day ahead. This service has no hymns and lasts around 30 minutes

9.30am | Wednesday morning

Every Wednesday morning at 9.30am we meet in church for a quiet celebration of the Eucharist. This service has no hymns and lasts around 30 minutes.

10.00am | Wednesday morning

Bible study with refreshments is held after service at 10.00am in the Billie and Leslie Norman Rooms.

Our Parish Prayer 

Heavenly Father, pour down your Spirit upon our church and parish. Grant us a new vision of your glory, a new experience of your power, a new faithfulness to your word, and a new consecration to your service; that through our renewed witness, your holy name may be glorified, And your Kingdom come. We ask this through Jesus Christ our Lord Who lives and reigns with you and The Holy Spirit, One God, World without end. Amen.

Prayer for St Michel du Valle (Vale Parish Church)

Saint Michael, the Archangel, defend us in battle, be our protection against the malice and snares of the devil. May God rebuke him, I humbly pray; and do thou, O Prince of the Heavenly host, by the power of God, thrust into hell Satan and all evil spirits who wander through the world for the ruin of souls. Amen

Safeguarding Policy

Vale Church Safeguarding Policy

As per Guernsey Deanery The Church Safeguarding Handbook, at Vale Church we are committed to…

  • Safeguarding all children, young people and vulnerable adults
  • Carefully selecting and training staff and volunteers, using the Disclosure and Barring Service amongst other tools, to check their suitability
  • Responding without delay to every complaint made which suggests that an adult, child or young person may have been harmed or is at risk
  • Cooperating fully with the police, local authority and any other appropriate statutory body in any investigation
  • Ministering appropriately to anyone who has experienced abuse
  • Extending pastoral care to offenders, whilst ensuring that children and vulnerable adults are protected from harm
